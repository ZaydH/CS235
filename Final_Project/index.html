The Olympic Rings

The rings stand for those colors that appeared on all the national flags that competed in the Olympic games in 1912

Jesse Owens in the 1936 Olympics

Jesse Owens won the 100m sprint, the 200m sprint, the long jump, the 4x100 sprint relay

Michael Phelps in the 2008 Olympics

In winning eight gold medals at the 2008 Beijing Games, Phelps took the record for the most first-place finishes at any single Olympic Games

The 2000 Olympic Games

Commonly known as Sydney 2000 or the Millennium Olympic Games/Games of the New Millennium

Michael Johnson in the 1996 Olympics

Johnson ran 19.66 seconds in the 200m at the U.S. Olympic Trials, At the 200m final, he finished the race in a world record time of 19.32 seconds

Usain Bolt in the 2008 Olympics

Winning in 9.69s (a world record), which was an improvement upon his own world record. Bolt visibly slowed down to celebrate before he finished

The 2008 Summer Olympic Games

Commonly known as Beijing 2008

United States Men's Olympic basketball team in the 1992 Olympics

Nicknamed the "Dream Team", the first American Olympic team with active NBA players.

The Olympic flame from the 2012 Olympics

Commonly known as London 2012

Welcome to Olympic History

The modern Olympic Games are the leading international sporting event featuring summer and winter sports competitions in which thousands of athletes from around the world participate in a variety of competitions. The Olympic Games are considered to be the world's foremost sports competition with more than 200 nations participating. The Olympic Games are held every four years, with the Summer and Winter Games alternating by occurring every four years but two years apart.
